# Quick Profile: Questionnaire

Answer honestly based on the last 2–3 months. There are no right answers — only real ones.

---

## 1. Your nervous system

*Three modes. Everyone has all three. The question is where you spend most of your time.*

Read the three descriptions below. Estimate what percentage of a typical day you spend in each. The total should roughly equal 100%.

**A — Calm engagement.** You can focus without forcing yourself. Being around people doesn't drain you. Your body is relaxed, your mind is clear. You can listen to someone and actually hear them.

**My percentage: _____%**

**B — On edge.** Restless, irritable, or anxious. Hard to stop, hard to relax. Tense shoulders, racing thoughts, trouble falling asleep. Constant feeling that something needs to be done.

**My percentage: _____%**

**C — Shut down.** Low energy, low motivation. The world feels muted. Scrolling your phone for hours, not because you enjoy it. Functioning on autopilot. Emotions are dulled.

**My percentage: _____%**

> A = Connection mode, B = Mobilization mode, C = Freeze mode

**When stress hits, what happens first?**

- [ ] I get activated — heart speeds up, urge to act or fight
- [ ] I shut down — go numb, foggy, want to hide
- [ ] I stay engaged and look for solutions

**After an hour of stress, where am I?**

- [ ] Still wired, can't calm down
- [ ] Crashed — no energy, scrolling, zoned out
- [ ] Mostly recovered

→ *Your nervous system pattern: notice where you spend the most time, and where stress takes you. [Go deeper](../model/layer-0-autonomic/)*

## 2. Sensing your body

*How well do you pick up signals from inside?*

Rate each from 1 (not me) to 5 (definitely me):

| # | Statement | 1–5 |
|---|-----------|-----|
| 1 | I notice when my breathing changes (faster, shallower, holding) | ___ |
| 2 | I can tell the difference between hungry and just wanting to eat | ___ |
| 3 | I feel stress in my body before I can name the emotion | ___ |
| 4 | I notice when my muscles are tense without someone pointing it out | ___ |
| 5 | I notice shifts in my energy throughout the day | ___ |

**Total: ___ / 25**

**Now a quick experiment.** Sit still, close your eyes, and try to feel your heartbeat from the inside — without touching your pulse. Count beats for 15 seconds. Then check against your actual pulse.

- [ ] Close (within a few beats) — your body sense is sharp
- [ ] Off by a lot or couldn't feel it — your body sense may be quiet (this is very common)

> A high score with low accuracy on the experiment may mean you pay a lot of attention to your body but interpret signals through thoughts rather than direct sensation. A low score with good accuracy may mean you have a strong channel that you don't use much yet. Both are useful information.

→ *Your interoceptive pattern: sharp sensor, quiet sensor, or somewhere between. [Go deeper](../model/layer-1-interoception/)*

## 3. Sensory processing

*How does your nervous system handle input from the environment?*

Rate each from 1 (almost never) to 5 (almost always):

| # | Statement | 1–5 |
|---|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Background noise bothers me when I'm trying to focus | ___ |
| 2 | Bright lights or flickering screens bother me more than they seem to bother others | ___ |
| 3 | Certain textures (clothing tags, rough fabrics) are hard to ignore | ___ |
| 4 | After a busy, noisy day I feel drained and need quiet to recover | ___ |
| 5 | I get restless or bored in quiet, low-stimulation environments | ___ |

**Total for items 1–4: ___ / 20** (sensitivity)
**Item 5: ___** (seeking)

- **Sensitivity 16–20:** Your system registers a lot. Overstimulation is a real risk for you.
- **Sensitivity 8–15:** Moderate — some things get through, others don't.
- **Sensitivity 4–7:** Your system has a high threshold. You may need more input to feel engaged.
- **Seeking 4–5:** You actively need stimulation to feel alive. Quiet environments may feel unbearable.

→ *Your sensory pattern: sensitive, seeking, mixed? [Go deeper](../model/layer-2-sensory/)*

## 4. Closeness with others

*How do you behave in close relationships — especially when things get uncertain?*

Rate each from 1 (strongly disagree) to 7 (strongly agree):

| # | Statement | 1–7 |
|---|-----------|-----|
| 1 | I worry about being abandoned by people I'm close to | ___ |
| 2 | When my partner doesn't reply quickly, I start imagining the worst | ___ |
| 3 | I find it hard to fully trust that people will stay | ___ |
| 4 | I prefer not to depend on others — I'd rather handle things myself | ___ |
| 5 | When someone gets too close, I feel an urge to pull back | ___ |
| 6 | I'm more comfortable with independence than intimacy | ___ |

**Anxiety score (items 1–3): ___ / 21**
**Avoidance score (items 4–6): ___ / 21**

- **Low anxiety + Low avoidance:** Security is relatively accessible to you. Closeness doesn't trigger panic or withdrawal.
- **High anxiety + Low avoidance:** You want closeness intensely but worry it will disappear. Relationships feel high-stakes.
- **Low anxiety + High avoidance:** You value autonomy strongly. Closeness can feel like a threat to your independence.
- **High anxiety + High avoidance:** You want closeness and fear it at the same time. Push-pull is your pattern.

> "High" and "low" are relative. These are tendencies, not types. And they can be different in different relationships.

→ *Your attachment pattern: what pulls you closer and what pushes you away. [Go deeper](../model/layer-3-attachment/)*

## 5. Stress load

*How much accumulated stress is your body carrying right now?*

Rate each from 0 (not at all) to 3 (most of the time / severely):

| # | Item | 0–3 |
|---|------|-----|
| 1 | I wake up tired even after enough sleep | ___ |
| 2 | I get sick more often than I used to | ___ |
| 3 | My sleep is disrupted | ___ |
| 4 | I have persistent muscle tension or pain | ___ |
| 5 | Brain fog — hard to focus, find words, hold things in memory | ___ |
| 6 | I feel emotionally flat or easily overwhelmed (or both, alternating) | ___ |
| 7 | Small problems feel big; I have less patience than I used to | ___ |

**Total: ___ / 21**

- **0–5:** Low load. Your system is recovering well.
- **6–10:** Moderate load. Some wear is showing. Good time to pay attention.
- **11–15:** High load. Multiple systems are signaling strain.
- **16–21:** Very high load. Please consider talking to a healthcare professional alongside these practices.

→ *Your current stress burden — and what drives it. [Go deeper](../model/layer-4-allostatic-load/)*

## Your profile at a glance

After completing all five sections, you have a snapshot:

1. **Nervous system:** Where do you spend your time? Where does stress take you?
2. **Body sensing:** How well do you hear your body's signals?
3. **Sensory processing:** How sensitive is your system to the environment?
4. **Closeness:** What happens when intimacy gets close or uncertain?
5. **Stress load:** How much is your body carrying right now?

This is not a score to optimize. It's a map of where you are today. Some of it you can change, some of it you work with. The next step is [practices](practices.md) — concrete things you can start doing this week.
