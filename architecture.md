# Architecture / Архитектура

## What is a layer / Что такое слой

InnerWire is built as a stack of independent layers. Each layer describes one aspect of how your body and mind work — backed by a specific field of research. Layers don't depend on each other: you can use any single layer and get value. But together they form a richer picture.

InnerWire построен как стек независимых слоёв. Каждый слой описывает один аспект работы тела и психики, опираясь на конкретную область исследований. Слои не зависят друг от друга: любой из них полезен сам по себе. Но вместе они дают более полную картину.

## The five layers / Пять слоёв

| Layer | Name | What it measures | Scientific basis | Status |
|-------|------|------------------|------------------|--------|
| 0 | Autonomic Profile | Three modes of the nervous system (connection, mobilization, freeze) | Polyvagal Theory (Porges), HRV research | 🟡 draft |
| 1 | Interoceptive Profile | How well you sense and interpret body signals | Barrett, Craig, Critchley & Garfinkel | 🟡 draft |
| 2 | Sensory Profile | How you process sensory input from the environment | Dunn's Sensory Processing Framework | 🟡 draft |
| 3 | Attachment Pattern | How you build and maintain closeness with others | Bowlby, Ainsworth, Bartholomew, ECR-R | 🟡 draft |
| 4 | Allostatic Load | Cumulative stress burden on body systems | McEwen, Sterling, Juster | 🟡 draft |

## Layer ordering rationale / Почему такой порядок

The layers are ordered from most physiological (bottom) to most psychological (top), and from least conscious to most conscious:

- **Layer 0** (Autonomic) — operates below awareness, milliseconds
- **Layer 1** (Interoception) — body signals reaching awareness
- **Layer 2** (Sensory) — processing external stimuli
- **Layer 3** (Attachment) — relational patterns, shaped over years
- **Layer 4** (Allostatic Load) — cumulative result of all the above over time

This is not a hierarchy of importance. It's a progression from "what your body does automatically" to "what accumulates over your lifetime."

## How layers interact / Как слои взаимодействуют

Layers are independent but influence each other:

- **0 → 1:** Your autonomic state affects interoceptive accuracy (hard to sense subtle body signals in mobilization mode)
- **1 → 0:** Better interoception helps regulate autonomic states
- **0 → 3:** Autonomic patterns shaped by early attachment experiences
- **2 → 0:** Sensory overload can trigger mobilization or freeze
- **All → 4:** Chronic dysregulation in any layer contributes to allostatic load
- **4 → All:** High allostatic load degrades functioning across all layers

These connections will be documented in cross-reference sections as layers mature.

## Depth levels / Уровни глубины

Layers are vertical slices — each covers a different aspect (nervous system, perception, attachment...). But InnerWire also has horizontal slices — depth levels that cut across all layers. Each level gives you a complete picture across all five axes, just at different depth and detail.

Слои — это вертикальные срезы: каждый покрывает отдельный аспект (нервная система, восприятие, привязанность...). Но InnerWire также нарезается горизонтально — уровни глубины, которые проходят сквозь все слои. Каждый уровень даёт целостную картину по всем пяти осям, просто с разной детализацией.

### Mirror / Зеркало

Quick profile across all axes. Short questionnaire, recognizable patterns, 2–3 starter practices per axis. Full path takes ~20 minutes. No theory, no jargon, no citations — just self-recognition and first steps.

Быстрый профиль по всем осям. Короткий опросник, узнаваемые паттерны, 2–3 стартовые практики на каждую ось. Весь путь — ~20 минут. Никакой теории, терминологии, ссылок — только узнавание себя и первые шаги.

**Audience:** anyone curious about themselves. Entry point for the whole system.

### Map / Карта

Understanding the mechanics. Why you get stuck in a pattern, how axes influence each other, full questionnaires, detailed practices with explanations of why they work. Scientific terms appear but are always explained. This is the core content of each layer's description.md, questionnaire.md, and practices.md.

Понимание механики. Почему ты застреваешь в паттерне, как оси влияют друг на друга, полные опросники, развёрнутые практики с объяснением, почему они работают. Научные термины есть, но всегда объясняются. Это основной контент description.md, questionnaire.md и practices.md каждого слоя.

**Audience:** people who want to understand, not just recognize.

### Lab / Лаборатория

Full scientific basis. Theories, criticism, methodological debates, primary sources. Collapsible `<details>` sections in layer content, sources.md, and research/ directory.

Полная научная база. Теории, критика, методологические дебаты, первоисточники. Разделы `<details>` в контенте слоёв, sources.md и директория research/.

**Audience:** professionals, researchers, anyone who wants to verify every claim.

### How levels relate / Как уровни связаны

Depth levels are **views**, not separate content. Mirror draws from the same material as Map and Lab — it just shows less of it. A reader can move from Mirror to Map to Lab seamlessly, going deeper into whatever caught their attention.

Уровни глубины — это **представления**, а не отдельный контент. «Зеркало» берёт из того же материала, что «Карта» и «Лаборатория» — просто показывает меньше. Читатель может двигаться от «Зеркала» к «Карте» и «Лаборатории» плавно, углубляясь в то, что его зацепило.

## File structure / Структура файлов

### Mirror level / Уровень «Зеркало»

Lives in a single cross-cutting directory, not per-layer:

Живёт в одной сквозной директории, а не по слоям:

```
quick-profile/
├── README.md          # What this is, how to use it (~2 min read)
├── questionnaire.md   # Compact questionnaire across all 5 axes (~15 min)
└── practices.md       # Starter practices for each axis (2–3 per axis)
```

### Map level / Уровень «Карта»

Per-layer content (existing structure):

Контент по слоям (текущая структура):

```
layer-N-name/
├── README.md          # Quick start: 3 steps, connection to other layers
├── description.md     # Core concepts, daily-life manifestations, patterns
├── questionnaire.md   # Full self-observation tool (NOT a clinical test)
├── practices.md       # Detailed exercises for each pattern
└── sources.md         # Key citations + link to research/
```

### Lab level / Уровень «Лаборатория»

Deep-dive content within layers + shared research directory:

Углублённый контент внутри слоёв + общая директория исследований:

```
layer-N-name/
└── deep-dive.md       # Full theory, criticism, methodological notes

research/
├── bibliography.md    # Annotated bibliography across all layers
└── sources-layer-N.md # Detailed source reviews per layer
```

See `templates/layer-template/` for the canonical structure.

## Design principles / Принципы проектирования

### Spectrums, not types
Every layer uses continuous axes, not discrete categories. When we name "patterns" or "zones," these are convenient labels for regions of a continuous space — not boxes to put people in.

### Self-contained layers
A reader should be able to read one layer, do the questionnaire, try the practices, and get value — without reading anything else.

### Honest about limitations
Every layer's description.md includes a section on criticism and limitations of the underlying theory. We don't hide inconvenient research.

### Practical
Theory is only useful if it leads to action. Every layer includes concrete practices matched to the reader's self-observed pattern.

### Agency over identity
Your profile is a starting point, not a verdict. InnerWire shows where you are, what shaped it, and what you can change. The reader is the author — the system provides tools, not labels.
